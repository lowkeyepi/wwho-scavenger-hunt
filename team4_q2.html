Mpox, formerly known as monkeypox, is a viral disease caused by the monkeypox virus, a member of the Orthopoxvirus genus (the same family as smallpox). First discovered in laboratory monkeys in 1958, the disease was historically endemic to Central and West Africa. In 2022, mpox gained global attention when it spread to over 100 countries outside of Africa, primarily affecting communities that had not previously experienced the disease. The virus spreads through close contact with infected individuals, contaminated materials, or infected animals. Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle aches, and a characteristic rash that progresses through several stages. While most cases are mild, the disease can be more severe in children, pregnant individuals, and immunocompromised people.
