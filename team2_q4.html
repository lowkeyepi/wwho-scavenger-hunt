COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which emerged in late 2019 in Wuhan, China. The virus quickly spread globally, leading to a pandemic that profoundly affected public health, economies, and daily life worldwide. SARS-CoV-2 is primarily transmitted through respiratory droplets and aerosols when infected individuals cough, sneeze, talk, or breathe. The virus belongs to the coronavirus family, which also includes viruses that cause the common cold as well as more severe diseases like SARS and MERS. COVID-19 symptoms range from mild (fever, cough, fatigue) to severe (pneumonia, respiratory failure). The pandemic accelerated vaccine development, with multiple effective vaccines developed in record time using various technologies including mRNA platforms. The virus continues to evolve through mutations, producing new variants that may have different transmissibility or severity.
