Team 1 - Answer Key
Question 1: H5N1 Avian Influenza
What is the primary reason public health officials are concerned about H5N1 avian influenza?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: C) The virus could mutate to enable efficient human-to-human transmission
The main concern is that H5N1 could mutate to allow efficient human-to-human transmission, potentially causing a pandemic. While current human cases are rare and usually linked to direct bird contact, influenza viruses can evolve rapidly.
Question 2: Mpox
What makes mpox a "re-emerging" infectious disease rather than a new disease?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) It has existed for decades but recently spread to new geographic areas
Mpox is considered re-emerging because it has been known since 1958 and was endemic in parts of Africa, but in 2022 it spread globally to regions where it had never been seen before, affecting new populations.
Question 3: Ebola
Which of the following is the most important factor in preventing Ebola transmission in healthcare settings?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Using proper personal protective equipment (PPE) and infection control practices
Proper PPE and strict infection control practices are critical for preventing Ebola transmission in healthcare settings. Since Ebola spreads through direct contact with bodily fluids, healthcare workers must use appropriate protective gear and follow rigorous protocols.
Question 4: COVID-19
What is one key reason why COVID-19 was able to spread so rapidly around the world compared to previous coronavirus outbreaks like SARS (2003)?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) People with COVID-19 can spread the virus before showing symptoms (pre-symptomatic transmission)
Pre-symptomatic and asymptomatic transmission made COVID-19 much harder to control than SARS. People could unknowingly spread the virus before feeling sick, making it difficult to identify and isolate cases early. With SARS, people were typically contagious only after becoming visibly ill.
Question 5: Dengue Fever
Why is dengue fever considered an emerging threat in new geographic regions?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Climate change and urbanization are expanding the range of mosquito vectors
Climate change is creating warmer temperatures in previously cooler regions, allowing Aedes mosquitoes to survive and breed in new areas. Combined with urbanization and increased travel, dengue is spreading to regions where it was previously uncommon or absent.
Question 6: Drug-Resistant Tuberculosis
What is the primary cause of drug-resistant tuberculosis developing?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Patients not completing their full course of TB treatment
When patients don't complete their full course of TB treatment (which typically lasts 6-9 months for regular TB), some bacteria survive and can develop resistance to the drugs. This is why it's crucial for TB patients to take all their medications exactly as prescribed for the entire treatment period.
Team 2 - Answer Key
Question 1: H5N1 Avian Influenza
What animal is considered the natural reservoir for H5N1 avian influenza?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Wild aquatic birds (like ducks and geese)
Wild aquatic birds are the natural reservoir for avian influenza viruses, including H5N1. They can carry the virus without getting sick and spread it to domestic poultry through contact or contaminated water sources.
Question 2: Mpox
How is mpox primarily transmitted between humans?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Through close physical contact with infected individuals or contaminated materials
Mpox spreads through close contact with infected individuals, their lesions or scabs, bodily fluids, or contaminated materials like bedding. It requires close physical contact, unlike diseases that spread through the air over long distances.
Question 3: Ebola
Which animal is believed to be the natural reservoir host for Ebola virus?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Fruit bats
Fruit bats are believed to be the natural reservoir for Ebola virus. They can carry the virus without getting sick. Humans usually become infected through contact with infected bats or animals that have been infected by bats.
Question 4: COVID-19
What type of vaccine technology was used in the Pfizer and Moderna COVID-19 vaccines?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: C) mRNA (messenger RNA)
The Pfizer and Moderna vaccines use mRNA technology, which teaches cells to make a harmless piece of the spike protein that triggers an immune response. This was the first time mRNA vaccines were deployed at large scale for humans.
Question 5: Dengue Fever
How many distinct serotypes of dengue virus exist?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: C) 4
There are four distinct dengue virus serotypes (DENV-1, DENV-2, DENV-3, DENV-4). Infection with one serotype provides lifelong immunity to that specific serotype, but only short-term immunity to the others. This is important because subsequent infections with different serotypes increase the risk of severe dengue.
Question 6: Drug-Resistant Tuberculosis
Approximately how long does treatment for multidrug-resistant TB (MDR-TB) typically take?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: D) 18-24 months or longer
MDR-TB treatment typically takes 18-24 months or even longer, compared to 6-9 months for regular TB. The treatment is more complex, expensive, and involves medications with more serious side effects, making adherence challenging.
Team 3 - Answer Key
Question 1: H5N1 Avian Influenza
How do most human H5N1 infections occur?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Direct contact with infected birds or contaminated environments
Most human H5N1 cases result from direct contact with sick or dead infected poultry, or exposure to contaminated environments like live bird markets. The virus does not spread easily between people, and properly cooked poultry products are safe to eat.
Question 2: Mpox
What is a key characteristic of the mpox rash?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) It progresses through multiple stages from macules to pustules to scabs
The mpox rash is distinctive because it progresses through several stages: flat spots (macules), raised bumps (papules), fluid-filled blisters (vesicles), pus-filled lesions (pustules), and finally scabs. This progression helps with diagnosis.
Question 3: Ebola
What is the typical incubation period for Ebola (time between infection and symptoms)?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) 2-21 days (average 8-10 days)
Ebola has an incubation period of 2-21 days, with symptoms typically appearing around 8-10 days after exposure. During the incubation period, people are not contagious - they only become infectious once symptoms develop.
Question 4: COVID-19
What does "R0" (R-naught) measure in infectious disease epidemiology?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) The average number of people one infected person will infect in a completely susceptible population
R0 (basic reproduction number) represents the average number of secondary infections caused by one infected person in a completely susceptible population. For COVID-19, variants have had different R0 values, with some variants being more transmissible than others.
Question 5: Dengue Fever
What time of day are Aedes aegypti mosquitoes (the primary dengue vector) most active?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Early morning and late afternoon/evening
Aedes aegypti mosquitoes are most active during early morning (after dawn) and in the late afternoon/evening (before dusk). Unlike many other mosquito species, they bite during the day, which is why daytime mosquito protection is important in dengue-endemic areas.
Question 6: Drug-Resistant Tuberculosis
What strategy helps ensure TB patients complete their treatment and prevents drug resistance?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: A) DOT (Directly Observed Therapy) where healthcare workers watch patients take their medicine
Directly Observed Therapy (DOT) is a key strategy where healthcare workers or trained volunteers directly observe patients taking their TB medications. This ensures adherence to treatment, which is critical for cure and preventing the development of drug-resistant TB.
Team 4 - Answer Key
Question 1: H5N1 Avian Influenza
What makes H5N1 different from seasonal flu viruses?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) It has a much higher mortality rate in humans who do get infected
While H5N1 rarely infects humans, when it does, the mortality rate is approximately 50%, much higher than seasonal flu which has a mortality rate below 1%. However, H5N1 does not currently spread easily between people.
Question 2: Mpox
Why did the WHO recommend changing the name from "monkeypox" to "mpox" in 2022?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) To reduce stigma and avoid discriminatory language
The WHO changed the name to mpox to reduce stigma and avoid discriminatory or racist language that emerged during the 2022 outbreak. The goal was to use a neutral, non-stigmatizing name for the disease.
Question 3: Ebola
Why is it especially important to handle deceased Ebola victims carefully?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Bodies of Ebola victims remain highly infectious and require safe burial practices
Ebola virus remains infectious in the bodies of deceased victims. Traditional burial practices involving washing and touching the body can lead to transmission. Safe burial practices by trained personnel are critical for controlling Ebola outbreaks.
Question 4: COVID-19
What is "long COVID"?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Persistent symptoms or new health problems lasting weeks to months after initial COVID infection
Long COVID (also called post-acute sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection or PASC) refers to persistent symptoms or new health problems that continue for weeks, months, or even longer after the initial COVID-19 infection. Symptoms can include fatigue, brain fog, shortness of breath, and many others.
Question 5: Dengue Fever
What public health measure is most effective at controlling dengue at the community level?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) Eliminating standing water where mosquitoes breed
Eliminating mosquito breeding sites by removing standing water (in flower pots, tires, containers, etc.) is the most effective community-level intervention. Aedes mosquitoes breed in small amounts of standing water, so source reduction is key. While vaccines exist, they are not yet widely available or recommended for all populations.
Question 6: Drug-Resistant Tuberculosis
What is "latent TB infection"?
‚úì CORRECT ANSWER: B) When TB bacteria are in the body but not causing illness or symptoms
Latent TB infection occurs when a person has TB bacteria in their body but the bacteria are inactive and don't cause symptoms. People with latent TB are not contagious. However, the bacteria can become active later in life, especially if the immune system weakens. About 5-10% of people with latent TB will develop active TB if not treated.
