H5N1 is a highly pathogenic avian influenza virus that primarily affects birds but can occasionally infect humans who have close contact with infected poultry. First identified in 1996 in geese in China, H5N1 has caused sporadic human infections with a high mortality rate (around 50%). The virus spreads among birds through direct contact and contaminated environments. While human-to-human transmission is extremely rare, public health officials closely monitor H5N1 because influenza viruses can mutate and potentially cause pandemics. Recent outbreaks in wild birds and poultry farms across multiple continents have raised concerns about food security and pandemic preparedness.
